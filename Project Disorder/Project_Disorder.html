Project Disorder - VR Parkinson's Diagnosis

My team developed a VR-based diagnostic application designed to streamline the early detection and severity assessment of Parkinson's disease in collaboration with the University of California San Diego Medical Center. The project transformed traditional clinical evaluation methods into an immersive, data-driven experience. Conventional diagnosis often requires neurologists to spend up to an hour observing motor symptoms, administering movement tests, and assessing tremor severity. This project leveraged medical research and motion analysis techniques to reduce the process to under five minutes through a series of interactive VR simulations.

Using VR controllers and headset tracking, the application captures precise tremor, movement, and reaction data while patients complete guided tasks designed to replicate standard neurological assessments. The backend system processes this biometric and motion data in real time, analyzing tremor frequency, amplitude, hand stability, and motor response patterns to estimate disease presence and severity. The platform was built to improve diagnostic efficiency, increase consistency in patient evaluations, and provide clinicians with objective quantitative data to support medical decision-making. By combining immersive technology, real-time analytics, and healthcare research, the project demonstrates how XR solutions can modernize patient assessment workflows and enhance accessibility to neurological screening tools.
